# COMMERCIAL OPERATING SYSTEM
## Internal operating manual — semiconductor commercial organizations

**Contents**
1. Customer Operating Models — how the people who buy semiconductors actually decide
2. Commercial Mechanisms — the nine systems that create and destroy commercial advantage
3. Commercial Archetypes — the recurring machines, their economics, and what breaks them
4. Operating Environments — six regional commercial systems
5. Commercial Judgment — deciding with incomplete information

**Notation.** [F] established fact, grounded in public record (filings, investor communications, product and standards history). [I] reasonable inference; the chain from fact is stated or evident. [O] observation; consistent across experienced practitioners; reliable for how things work, not for exact figures. [H] hypothesis; identified as such; never a sufficient basis for action. Figures appear as ranges. Companies appear as evidence for mechanisms, never as the subject.
