## **5 Key Takeaways**

1. Customers don't buy chips, they buy reduced risk. The real decision is whose failure will cost them the least.
2. Design wins drive everything. Almost every commercial decision — where you put people, how you price, what you support — ultimately comes down to winning and holding the right sockets.
3. There is no single global semiconductor market. North America, Europe, Japan, Taiwan, Korea, and Mainland China each operate under different commercial rules and buyer behaviors. You have to adapt your execution without changing your core model.
4. Sustainable advantage comes from systems that compound over time. Trust, qualification walls, switching costs, and ecosystems either reinforce themselves or unravel quickly once they start to weaken.
5. Good commercial judgment is about handling uncertainty with discipline. You need to clearly separate what you know, what you believe, and what you're assuming, then decide what evidence would actually change your mind.
